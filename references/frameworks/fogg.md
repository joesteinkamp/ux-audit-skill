## The model
**B = MAP** — a behavior happens when **Motivation**, **Ability**, and a **Prompt**
converge at the same moment. When a target behavior (signup, purchase, activation)
is not happening, exactly one of those three is missing.

| Element | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Motivation | The drive to perform the behavior; can compensate for low ability. |
| Ability | How easy the behavior is at that moment; simplicity beats motivation boosts. |
| Prompt | The cue that triggers the behavior; without it nothing happens. |

## Why this is not evaluated
A screenshot can show a weak prompt (CTA below the fold), an ability barrier (a long
form), or a missing value proposition — but whether any of those explains a conversion
gap is only knowable from behavioral data: funnel analytics, viewability instrumentation,
A/B tests, exit surveys. Asserting Fogg diagnoses from static images would be exactly
the unverifiable feedback this skill is designed to avoid.

When visible evidence alone is a real violation (e.g. the CTA fails contrast, the form
has unlabeled fields), the Tier-1/2 frameworks already catch it under their own
principles — nothing is lost by excluding Fogg from scoring.
